# Harmonious Day: A Daily Balance Between Deen & Dao

## 1. Wu Xing (Five Phases) Rhythm
The day is a continuous cycle of five dynamic phases of energy (Wu Xing), not static elements. Aligning activities with these phases creates a natural, self-supporting flow.

### WOOD PHASE (05:30-09:00)
**Element**: Growth, Planning, Vitality - Spring energy, new beginnings  
**Islamic Anchor**: Fajr - divine start, remembrance, intention
**Core Quality & Purpose**: Planning, new beginnings, vitality
**Ideal Activities**: Setting intentions, dynamic movement, strategic planning
**Sequence**:
1. Fajr & Centering (~60m): Salah, stillness, intention setting (Tummo, Makko Ho, meditation, reading)
2. Wooden Movement (~60m): Dynamic "awakening" movement (running, stretching, circuit training, Ji Jin Jing)
3. Nourishment & Planning (~30m): Breakfast, daily planning
**Rule**: Spiritual centering → Movement → Nourishment

### FIRE PHASE (09:00-13:00)  
**Element**: Energy, Expression, Focus - Summer energy
**Core Quality & Purpose**: Peak output, creativity, radiance
**Ideal Activities**: Deep work, creative execution, challenging tasks

### EARTH PHASE (13:00-15:00)
**Element**: Integration - Late Summer pivot point  
**Islamic Anchor**: Zuhr - grounding
**Core Quality & Purpose**: Stability, nourishment, grounding
**Ideal Activities**: Meals, administrative tasks, processing information
**Sequence**:
1. Zuhr & Lunch (~45m): Prayer first, then time for a big meal
2. Post-lunch Movement (~30m): Short walk, Ba Duan Jin
3. Grounding Practice (~45m): A physically active, simple task or a habit like sketching/meditation 

### METAL PHASE (15:00-18:00)
**Element**: Letting Go - Autumn refinement
**Islamic Anchor**: Asr - reflection
**Core Quality & Purpose**: Precision, organization, completion
**Ideal Activities**: Structuring, refining, emails, study, letting go of the non-essential

### WATER PHASE (18:00-21:45)
**Element**: Rest - Winter introspection  
**Islamic Anchors**: Maghrib - gratitude & Isha - completion
**Core Quality & Purpose**: Storage, wisdom, restoration
**Ideal Activities**: Reflection, low-energy tasks, reading, wind-down routines
**Sequence**:
1. Maghrib & Movement (~90m): Maghrib + dinner → Walking or heavy exercise → Xi Sui Jing/Hatha Yoga
2. Quiet Time (~90m): Good for low-energy work such as household, study or creative tasks, or a reading habit if there are no tasks
3. Isha & Deep Wind-Down (21:00): Prayer → journaling, Yoga Nidra or meditation → sleep
**Non-negotiable**: Sleep by 21:45.

## Wu Wei (Effortless Action)
Productivity arises from acting in harmony with the natural flow, not from forceful striving. It is "effortless effort" or "doing by not-doing," where actions feel natural and spontaneous. When the schedule is aligned with the Wu Xing rhythm, the day unfolds with less friction and more ease.

## Sacred Rhythm (Islamic Prayer Anchors)
The five daily prayers (Salah) serve as immovable spiritual pillars that structure the day. They provide regular pauses for remembrance (Dhikr) and reflection, creating a sacred rhythm that grounds temporal pursuits in a divine context. These anchors take precedence in scheduling, marking the primary transitions between phases.

## Jar Prioritization (Stones, Pebbles, Sand)
This metaphor defines the non-negotiable order for filling the "jar" of your day.
1.  **Stones**: Fixed calendar events and critical, high-urgency tasks. These are scheduled first and are immovable.
2.  **Anchors**: Prayer times are scheduled around Stones.
3.  **Pebbles**: Important but flexible tasks. These are scheduled in the remaining space after Stones and Anchors.
4.  **Sand**: Habits, low-urgency chores, and self-care. These fill any remaining gaps.

## Balance (Mīzān)
The schedule must incorporate a daily and weekly variety of activities to prevent stagnation and burnout. This means balancing:
-   **Mental & Physical**
-   **Creative & Analytical**
-   **Social & Solitary**
A day of only one type of activity, even if productive, leads to imbalance.

## Sustainable Boundaries
-   **Realistic Capacity**: Acknowledge the limit of 6-10 hours of focused work per day.
-   **Sleep Protection**: The 21:45 sleep time is non-negotiable for restoring Water phase energy.
-   **Task Chunking**: Large tasks (>2 hours) are scheduled in blocks of 30-180 minutes to maintain focus and feasibility.

## SCHEDULING RULES

### 1. IMMOVABLE BLOCKS (CALENDAR EVENTS)
- Fixed events override all tasks & habits
- No entry may overlap a Calendar Event
- If overlap occurs → task is moved before/after
- If a prayer anchor overlaps a Calendar Event → prayer is skipped that day

### 2. CORE SCHEDULING ORDER
A → **Anchors**  
B → **Urgent/Difficult Tasks**  
C → **Chores & Habits**  
- Place prayers first (skipped only if blocked by fixed events)
- Fit as many urgent/difficult tasks as possible into realistic work capacity
- Remaining time is for habits/chores

### 3. PHASE ALIGNMENT (NON-NEGOTIABLE)
- Tasks must respect their elemental suitability 
- The best element for task is ONLY determined by what action the task requires
- The element to which a task belongs has NOTHING to do at all with urgency of the task 
- Habits must stay near their ideal phase + respect sequence rules
- Habits may be skipped when time is insufficient

### 4. CORE CONSTRAINTS
- No task or habit overlaps prayer or Calendar Event
- Entries must be cut/split if pressing into next event
- Nothing before “current time”
- Nothing after **21:45** and before **5:30**
