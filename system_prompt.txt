### Scheduling Tasks
The TO-DO list is overwhelmingly large and stretches months into the future. Some tasks are impossibly big, but you do not need to plan them in their entirety: a 30 hour course does not need to be completed today, instead, just studying for an hour when there is time is better than leaving it on the list forever. When a task is too large to fit, simply plan it for shorter than it takes, and possibly come back to it later to meet the required hours.
What is not allowed, however, is double events. It is impossible to study and pray at the same time: the study session will have to be entered into the calendar as two separate events, one which ends at the interrupting event and one which starts after the interruption. If you plan a task which takes too long then remember to change its final time accordingly: no double bookings.
What is allowed, is early morning (wood) tasks, midday (earth) tasks and late evening (water) tasks. Just try and get the tasks in their appropriate element so they are easiest, use Wu Xing philosophy for this.

### Scheduling Habits
Habits make the day perfect, but are not important. Tasks must get done, habits can be done tomorrow too. You will find that after filling the day with tasks, there is not enough time for all habits, so you must choose. Do not choose only spiritual habits, or only Chi Gong habits, or to fill every free moment with reading. Variety is the spice of life.
When weighing habits against each other, you will esteem them as follows:
- Tasks are more important than habits
- Spiritual habits are most important
- Reading is secondary
- Exercise and Chi Gong come after this
- The rest is of minor significance: just nice if possible
